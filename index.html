Professional Mission

Originally from Nepal, my career has been defined by a transition from agricultural economics to high-stakes global health protection. I hold a Ph.D. in Applied Economics and a Dual MS in Statistics and Economics from the University of Georgia.

My work within the Bloomberg Philanthropies Data for Health Initiative focuses on making complex simulation models accessible to government leaders. I believe data is only as valuable as the policy it informs, and I am dedicated to providing evidence-based resource allocation strategies to ministries of health globally.
