Chandra Dhakal, PhD

Transforming Complex Data into Actionable Global Health Policy

Executive Summary

Health Economist at the CDC Foundation, supporting the Division of Global Health Protection at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), Atlanta, GA, USA. With over 10 years of experience, I bridge the gap between rigorous quantitative research and real-world policy. My mission is to translate data into actionable interventions that improve lives globally.

BACKGROUND & PHILOSOPHY

Originally from Nepal, my career has spanned Kathmandu, Texas, and Georgia. My journey is defined by a transition from agricultural economics to the high-stakes intersection of statistics and global health economics.

"I believe data is only as valuable as the policy it informs. My work focuses on making complex simulation models accessible to government leaders to ensure evidence-based resource allocation."
