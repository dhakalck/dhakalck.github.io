Chandra Dhakal, PhD

Transforming Complex Data into Actionable Global Health Policy

Executive Summary

I am a Health Economist at the CDC Foundation, supporting the Division of Global Health Protection. With over 10 years of experience, I bridge the gap between rigorous quantitative research and real-world policy.

The Mission: Driven by a commitment to translate research into policy to improve the lives of those affected by chronic diseases, specifically through the Data to Policy (D2P) program.

Core Value Proposition: Expertise in combining microeconomic theories with structural econometric models to analyze health and food policy issues using massive household and retail scanner datasets.

Background & Philosophy

Originally from Nepal, I have built a global career spanning Kathmandu, Lubbock, and Atlanta. My journey is defined by a strategic transition from agricultural economics to the high-impact intersection of statistics and global health economics.

"I believe data is only as valuable as the policy it informs. My work focuses on making complex simulation models accessible to government leaders to ensure evidence-based resource allocation."
